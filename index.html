Scarlet Radiance

  • Engine: Construct 2
  • Platform(s): PC
  • Development time: 1 month
  • Other team members: None

Scarlet Radiance is a relaxing and minimalistic Metroidvania, with some light puzzle phases. You play as Scarlet, a shadowish form followed by devoted spirits, as you explore an empty and ancient temple. You can learn new abilities to access new areas, in which you can collect keys needed to open the final door. The game revolve implicitly around the serious themes of seclusion and suicide.

This is my first end-of-year project, made alone to challenge myself. Now considered as a prototype, as a brand new version is in pre-production phase.

Dahlia Noir

  • Engine: Unity
  • Platform(s): PC
  • Development time: > 48 hours
  • Roles: Game Designer, Sound Designer, Narration
  • Other team members:
    • Tiphaine Hardy
    • Morgane Moenner
    • Raphaël Giaccaglia
    • Emma Ledru

Dahlia Noir is a sound-based visual novel in which you play Dahlia, an investigator who has to question three suspects to solve a murder case. The music is the key to progress through the story: every suspect is linked to a musical instrument, and every choice correspond to a short loop which will be added over the background music as long as make the right choices. The complete music will be rebuilt when the case is solved.

Made during the Global Game Jam 2020 on the "Rebuild" theme. There is only half of the story integrated and minor tweaks needed due the limited time, but it is planned to finish it.

La Ferme!

  • Engine: Construct 2
  • Platform(s): PC - Web - Mobile - NoMad Arcade
  • Development time: 5 days
  • Roles: Game Designer, Developer, Sound Designer
  • Other team members:
    • Manon Ha-Thanh Gore-Koenig

La Ferme! (Shut the f**k up!) is a kind of shooter game in which you play Mémé, a granny who is annoyed by the noise coming from her neighborhood. She has to throw items on the building windows within the allocated time in order to make calm reign. Mémé has several projectiles at her disposal, each of them needed for different situations.

Made in a week, and was intended to join the NoMad Arcade game list before the project has been abandonned, though the game is fully compatible with this kind of arcade cabinet.

Nantes Marine Astronomical Observatory

Modern VR Visit

  • Engine: Unity
  • Platform(s): PC Headsets - Android
  • Roles: Game/Features Designer, Sound Designer, Developer
  • Other team members:
    • Hugo Richard
    • Kilian Hemery
    • Ségolène Fallourd
    • Manon Ha-Thanh Gore-Koenig

Nantes Marine Astronomical Observatory(formerly named The Tower) is a project split in two Augmented Reality and two Virtual Reality projects made by e-artsup school students (Nantes, France). It consist in several experiences which permit to visit the historical building, closed to the public, and its surroundings. The project is meant to be exposed at the new Jules Verne museum which is estimated to be open in 2024.

The Modern VR Visit is a Virtual Reality visit of its current setting. Made with a SteamVR integration and 360° pictures, its main purpose is to offer a safe guided visit of the place along various infos about the different rooms. The whole experience is made to be the more accessible possible, and doesn't require anything else than a headset in its current form.

This project is meant to be merged with its 1850 counterpart to provide the best visit possible with the ability to change the time period in a blink of an eye.

Vice City Remaster

Vice City Remaster is a project aiming to rebuild the world of Vice City (Grand Theft Auto - Vice City) in Unity. It is split in different parts sharing a common goal, which is to offer a new youth to this cult game without the constraints of the original engine. To achieve this, the original models and textures are extracted from the game datas, then imported in Unity before receiving a remodeling and a complete retexturing, consisting in the first part of the project. The second part will include a huge work on the ambiance, cars and peds. Finally, the third and last part will be to be able to visit the city through several ways, including cars, bikes and planes.

Currently, only a small part of the city is available in its original form.
